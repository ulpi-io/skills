# Outreach Template Library (paste-ready, compliant)

Personalize each from `.ulpi/launch/positioning.md` — fill `[brackets]`, never send raw. Every template
asks for a **visit / try / honest feedback**, never a vote. Run the banned-phrase scan
(`compliance-modes.md`) before sending.

**These are written in Product Hunt voice as the reference set for a waves-allowed channel.** For another
waves-allowed platform (a directory launch, etc.), swap `Product Hunt` → the platform, `[PH link]` →
`[launch link]`, and the PH-specific timing (`12:01am PT`, `until midnight Pacific`, the 24-hour window)
for that platform's. **If the compliance mode forbids mobilization (e.g. Hacker News), do not use the
broadcast/wave templates at all** — use only a genuine 1:1 share with a few people who'd actually care.

## Pre-launch

**Teaser DM (X / LinkedIn — top supporters, ~2 weeks out):**
> Hey [Name] — quick heads-up: I'm launching [Product] on Product Hunt next [Tuesday]. It's the thing
> I've been building in public — [one line: what it does / who it's for]. No ask today, just wanted you on
> the early list. When it goes live I'd genuinely love your honest first impression — the rough edges as
> much as the good. Want me to ping you when it's up?

**Waitlist / existing-users email (~3–5 days out):**
> **Subject:** We go live on Product Hunt this [Tuesday] — your honest take would mean a lot
>
> Hi [First name],
>
> This [Tuesday] at 12:01am PT, [Product] launches on Product Hunt. You've been with us early, so I
> wanted you to hear it from me first. Here's what's new since you last saw it: [1–2 concrete
> improvements].
>
> When we go live I'll send the link. All I'm hoping for is your honest reaction — try it, and tell me in
> the comments what clicked and what felt confusing. Real feedback (including the critical kind) is worth
> more to me than anything. I'll be in the thread all day.
>
> Thanks for being here from the start, [Name]
>
> *P.S. Brand-new Product Hunt accounts barely register on the site — so if you already have one, even
> better. But only if you actually find it useful.*

**Launch-eve reminder DM (night before):**
> Hey [Name] — we go live on Product Hunt tomorrow at 12:01am PT. Link to bookmark: [PH link]. If you get
> a sec tomorrow, I'd really value your honest first impression in the comments — good or brutal, both
> help. Thanks for being in the early crew!

## Launch day — personal & community

**Personal 1:1 DM (only to people you actually know — personalize each):**
> Hey [Name] — we launched [Product] on Product Hunt this morning and I thought of you because [genuine,
> specific reason: you hit this exact problem / you gave me the idea for X]. Would love your honest take if
> you have a sec: [link]. No worries if not — just wanted you to see it. 🙏

**Community heads-up (Slack / Discord / Indie Hackers — post ONCE, where you're a real member):**
> Hey all — [name] here, [long-time contributor]. After [context], we shipped [Product] today and it's
> live on Product Hunt → [PH link]. It does [one line], and I'd genuinely value feedback from this group
> specifically — you'll spot the gaps faster than anyone. Happy to answer anything here or on the launch.
> Not asking for anything beyond your honest take. 🙏
>
> *Rules check before posting: confirm the community allows launch/PH links; use the designated promo
> channel/day; do NOT ask for upvotes; do NOT DM the link to strangers.*

**X / Twitter launch thread (US-morning wave):**

> 👇 [PH link]
> **2/** The problem: [the painful status quo, ideally a story]. We were living this ourselves.

## DO / DON'T cheat sheet

**DO say:** "I'd love your honest feedback" · "take a look and tell me what's confusing" · "try it and
comment with your reaction" · "I'll be in the comments all day" · "we're live, here's the link".

**DON'T say:** "please upvote" · "support us" · "give us a vote" · "help us hit #1" · "an upvote would mean
everything" · "upvote and I'll give you [perk]" · mass-DM strangers · post the same plug in 10 communities
· email "direct upvote links".

**Rule of thumb:** ask people to **VISIT, TRY, and COMMENT** — never to **UPVOTE**. Never tie an incentive
to a vote. Let votes happen on their own.
