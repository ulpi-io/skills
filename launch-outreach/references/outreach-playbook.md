# Outreach Playbook (recruit · segment · wave)

How to build the audience, split it, and time the sends (Steps 1, 2, 4). Templates live in
`templates.md`; the per-platform rules live in `compliance-modes.md`.

## Recruit the audience (pre-launch)

You need a primed audience **before** launch — the platform amplifies existing momentum, it doesn't create
it. A commonly cited floor is **~100–300 warm contacts** (Demand Curve cites ≥400 as a target, not a
floor), and roughly 40–60% of launch-day engagement tends to come from your own audience.

Build it **off-platform** — several platforms (including Product Hunt) no longer offer a native pre-launch
"notify me" list:

- Stand up a simple **email-capture landing page** (Carrd / Beehiiv / ConvertKit / Mailchimp).
- **Build in public** on X/LinkedIn for 2–4 weeks (1–2 behind-the-scenes posts/week; demos, feedback asks).
- Be **genuinely active** in 3–5 communities you actually belong to, and on the launch platform itself
  (complete your profile; established, real accounts carry far more weight than brand-new ones).
- Line up **10–20 peers/founder friends** who'll genuinely check it out on the day.

Be honest with the user about reach: a launch with no audience underperforms — if there's no list, the
priority is building one, not blasting strangers.

## Segment & reach plan

| Segment | Who | Message | Timing |
|---------|-----|---------|--------|
| 1. Team / co-founders | the people shipping it | brief + on-call roles | before launch |
| 2. Friends & peers | people you know | personal, low-pressure heads-up | teaser, then day-of |
| 3. Existing users / waitlist | highest-intent group | "we're live — tell us what to fix" | eve + launch morning |
| 4. Communities | groups you're a *real* member of | one organic heads-up where allowed | day-of, once |
| 5. Public audience | X/LinkedIn followers | launch-day posts | day-of waves |

**Reach rule:** DM the **top ~20–50 supporters personally** (personalized each time — identical blasts to
strangers are prohibited mass-messaging). Reach the rest via **segmented email** and **broadcast posts**.

## Wave strategy (only where the compliance mode allows mobilization)

Stagger the day, don't blast at midnight. Staggering (1) sustains the steady velocity the ranking
algorithm reads as genuine interest across the full window, (2) avoids the spike pattern that triggers
gaming/spam detection (and vote removal), and (3) keeps a reserve for the midday lull and final hours.
Segment sends by engagement and timezone — EU/early-risers first, US-morning second, stragglers last.

A typical Pacific-time cadence (tune to your list):

- **12:05–12:20am** — closest circle only, via **personal 1:1 DMs**; one heads-up in 1–2 home communities.
- **12:30–2:00am** — **email Wave 1** to your most-engaged / EU / early-riser segment.
- **~6:00–9:00am** — US-morning push: post the **X thread**; **email Wave 2**; a **LinkedIn** post in the
  founder's voice.
- **~12:00pm (midday lull)** — pull your rank; if momentum slipped, fire **email Wave 3** (a held-back
  segment) + a "midday update" comment summarizing feedback themes.
- **~5:00–7:00pm** — evening push: a "what we've learned" social post; a final "last hours, live until
  midnight PT" email to non-openers.
- **~9:00–11:00pm** — final-hours rally to remaining supporters; a day-wrap thank-you.

> If the compliance mode **forbids** mobilization (e.g. Hacker News), there is no wave plan — coach being
> present and responsive in the thread, and share the link only with a few genuine peers who'd care.

## Team roles (if there's a team)

- **Maker/founder voice** — owns the first comment and all comment replies (voice consistency matters).
- **Comms/social** — runs the X thread, LinkedIn, and the scheduled email waves.
- **Community** — works the Slack/Discord/Reddit channels they personally belong to.
- **Rank-watcher** — monitors placement and owns the call to release the next wave.

Keep all vote-adjacent language out of every channel, in every role.
