# Compliance Modes (the per-platform dial)

The caller passes a **mode**; it overrides every default (Steps 0, 5). Each platform draws the line
between *announcing a launch / inviting engagement* (allowed) and *soliciting or manufacturing votes*
(prohibited) differently — get the mode right before drafting a single message.

## Mode: `product-hunt`

- **Mobilization:** **allowed, in waves.** You may notify your audience that you're live and invite them
  to visit, try, and comment. Stagger sends across the day (see `outreach-playbook.md`).
- **Hard rule:** never ask for or incentivize **upvotes**. PH: *"Don't ask for upvotes. Ask for feedback,
  comments, opinions, help spreading the word, not for upvotes."* Soliciting/incentivizing votes *"may
  trigger the algorithm to drop the product in the ranks or remove it from the homepage entirely."*
- **Email:** genuine, opt-in waitlist/customer email framed for feedback is fine. Cold mass-DM to
  strangers and scraped cold email are prohibited "mass messaging".
- **Offers:** a discount/perk for **everyone** is fine; a perk **conditioned on a vote** is prohibited.
- **Accounts:** real, complete, aged accounts only — never create or coordinate new/sock accounts to vote.

## Mode: `hacker-news`

- **Mobilization:** **forbidden.** Do **not** orchestrate supporter waves, "we're live, come engage"
  broadcasts, or any coordinated voting — HN penalizes vote rings harder than PH and can flag/penalize the
  post or ban the domain. **No wave plan.**
- **What's OK:** post the `Show HN` yourself, write a genuine first comment, and be **present and
  responsive** in the thread. Sharing the link with a few individual peers who'd genuinely care is fine;
  asking anyone to upvote is not.
- **Voice:** technical, humble, no hype, no emoji.

## Mode: `directory` / `generic`

- **Mobilization:** usually N/A (directories don't rank on supporter votes). Outreach is announcement +
  feedback only. Apply the universal banned-phrase scan regardless.

> Adding a platform? Define its mode here as: *mobilization allowed?* · *the bright-line prohibited act* ·
> *email rules* · *offer rules* · *account rules* — then the templates inherit it.

## The banned-phrase scan (run over every message before it ships)

Scan each message (case-insensitive). **Any hit → rewrite.**

`upvote`, `up-vote`, `up vote`, `vote for us`, `vote for me`, `give us a vote`, `support us` (in the
vote sense), `help us hit #1`, `help us win`, `an upvote would mean`, `smash that`, `hunt us up`, `click
to upvote`, any "do X and I'll give you Y" tied to a vote, and any plan to send **identical** copy to many
strangers.

Safe replacements: "I'd love your honest feedback" · "take a look and tell me what you think" · "try it
and comment with your reaction" · "we're live, here's the link" · "I'll be in the comments all day".

## Community self-promo rules (check before posting anywhere)

- Many communities **forbid or restrict** launch/promo links. Confirm the rules first; use the designated
  promo channel/day.
- Reddit norm: be an **established member** and keep a high genuine-to-promo ratio (community rules of
  thumb range from ~4:1 to the older reddiquette ~9:1 / "10% self-promo"). New-account link drops get
  removed or banned.
- Post **once**, where you genuinely participate, framed around the problem you solve — never the same
  plug across ten communities.
