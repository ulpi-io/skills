# Show HN — the Official Rules & Title Format

Load at Steps 2–3. All quotes are verbatim from the official `showhn.html`, `newsguidelines.html`, and
`newsfaq.html`, verified mid-2026.

## What qualifies as a Show HN

> **"Show HN is for something you've made that other people can play with. HN users can try it out, give
> you feedback, and ask questions in the thread."**

**On topic (qualifies):**
- *"things people can run on their computers or hold in their hands. For hardware, you can post a video or
  detailed article. For books, a sample chapter is ok."*
- *"The project should be non-trivial. Don't post quickly-generated one-offs… Share something that is
  deeply personal and interesting to you. Explain how and why."*
- *"The project must be something you've worked on personally and which you're around to discuss."*
- *"A Show HN needn't be complicated or look slick. The community is comfortable with work that's at an
  early stage."*
- *"Please make it easy for users to try your thing out, ideally without barriers such as signups or
  emails. You'll get more feedback that way."*

**Off topic (does NOT qualify — use a regular submission):**
- *"blog posts, sign-up pages, newsletters, lists, and other reading material. Those can't be tried out,
  so can't be Show HNs."*
- *"If your work isn't ready for users to try out, please don't do a Show HN… Don't post landing pages or
  fundraisers."*
- *"New features and upgrades ('Foo 1.3.1 is out') generally aren't substantive enough… A major overhaul
  is probably ok."*

(HN's literal disqualifier words are the list above — it does **not** use "waitlist" or "request for
help". A **video is allowed for hardware**.)

## The title

HN's only hard rule: *"To post, submit a story whose title begins with 'Show HN'."* The
**`Show HN: <name> – <plain description>`** shape is **strong convention**, not a quoted mandate — but
follow it. Most Show HN titles use a **space-padded en-dash `–` (U+2013)** as the separator, so prefer it —
but HN doesn't enforce or normalize any separator (it renders your literal title), and a plain hyphen `-`
shows up in real titles too. The en-dash is the dominant convention, not a rule; just don't use an em-dash `—`.

The general title rules (these **are** hard rules) from the Guidelines:
- *"Please don't do things to make titles stand out, like using uppercase or exclamation points, or
  saying how great an article is."*
- *"If the title includes the name of the site, please take it out."*
- *"If the title contains a gratuitous number or number + adjective… crop it. E.g. translate '10 Ways To
  Do X' to 'How To Do X', and '14 Amazing Ys' to 'Ys'."*
- *"please use the original title, unless it is misleading or linkbait; don't editorialize."*
- Append `[video]` or `[pdf]` if applicable.

Keep it ≤ ~80 chars, plain and specific. Works: *"Show HN: PromptTools – open-source tools for evaluating
LLMs and vector DBs"*; flops: a salesy rewrite.

## URL post vs text post, and the first comment

The norm is a **URL post** — the working thing *is* the link — then you add a top-level **first comment**
explaining what it is and why it's interesting (see `first-comment.md`). You can't inline links in a text
post: *"just submit the link, then add a regular comment."* (The "explain it in the first comment" practice
is convention + HN's linked tips thread, not a verbatim `showhn.html` line — but it's near-universal.)

## How a Show HN surfaces

*"Every Show HN appears on shownew. Once it clears a small points threshold, it will appear on the show
page in the top bar."* So: **submit → `/newest` + `/shownew` → clear a small points threshold → `/show`.**

## Reposting

*"If a story has not had significant attention in the last year or so, a small number of reposts is ok.
Otherwise we bury reposts as duplicates."* And *"Please don't delete and repost."* (It is **not** a literal
"repost exactly once" rule — see `postlaunch.md`.)

## No AI text

*"Don't post generated text or AI-edited text. HN is for conversation between humans."* Write the title,
first comment, and every reply yourself.

## Launch HN (YC-only — for context)

`Launch HN: <Company> (YC <Batch>) – <description>` (e.g. batch `YC P26` / `YC W25`) is the **YC-only**
equivalent: *"startup launches get placed on the front page"* as one of YC's three HN features. There's no
public Launch HN rulebook — YC/HN moderators coordinate it with founders directly. A non-YC product uses
**Show HN**.
