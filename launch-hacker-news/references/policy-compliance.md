# Hacker News Policy & Compliance

The compliance backbone — and HN is **materially stricter than Product Hunt**. Load when writing the post
(Step 3) and running the gate (Step 6). **This skill coaches genuine, compliant posting only.** If the
user asks to mobilize votes, decline and redirect here. All quotes are verbatim from official HN sources
(the Guidelines, the Show HN page, the FAQ) and moderator (dang) comments, verified mid-2026.

## The bright line: never solicit or coordinate votes

> **"Don't solicit upvotes, comments, or submissions. Users should vote and comment when they run across
> something they personally find interesting—not for promotion."** — Guidelines

> **"Please don't ask friends to upvote or comment. That's not ok on HN."** — Show HN

> FAQ — *Can I ask people to upvote my submission?* → *"No. … We penalize or ban submissions, accounts,
> and sites that break this rule, so please don't."*

Unlike PH (where supporter waves are allowed within limits), **HN forbids ALL vote mobilization.** No
asking friends, coworkers, your Slack/Discord, email list, or Twitter followers — not even a "we're live,
come check it out" aimed at driving votes. There is **no compliant version of mobilization on HN.**

## Why it backfires two ways

HN catches ring-voting with **both** software and the community:

- The **voting-ring detector** (a moderation priority for 12+ years) drops/penalizes coordinated votes —
  often **killing an otherwise-good post**. dang: *"It's sadly common for a great Show HN post to get
  demoted because its creators … tried to game it … the ring detector will nail their otherwise good post."*
- **Flagging** is a powerful counter-force: *"HN readers have gotten really good at noticing [rings] and
  then they get mad."* FAQ on asking for comments: *"HN readers are sensitive to this and will detect it,
  flag it, and use unkind words like 'spam'."*

The "growth hack" of linking to `/newest` on social to dodge the detector *"doesn't work and is blatantly
obvious."* As one summary put it: *"Upvote manipulation doesn't work on Hacker News … in contrast with
Product Hunt."*

## Prohibited (every one is a documented offense)

- [ ] Ask, hint, or remind **anyone** to upvote or comment (friends, team, users, communities, followers).
- [ ] Organize or join a **voting ring** (people upvoting each other's posts on cue).
- [ ] Use a second / throwaway / **sockpuppet** account to upvote or comment on your own post. *"Throwaway
      accounts are ok for sensitive information, but please don't create accounts routinely."*
- [ ] Link to `/newest` on social to funnel votes while dodging the detector.
- [ ] Buy votes/comments or use any service that supplies them.
- [ ] Accuse others of astroturfing/shilling/brigading in comments (separately against the guidelines).
- [ ] Use HN **primarily** for promotion: *"It's ok to post your own stuff part of the time, but the
      primary use of the site should be for curiosity."*

## Consequences ladder (least → most severe)

1. Votes silently dropped (*"Some votes are dropped by anti-abuse software"*).
2. Rank penalty / downweighting — the post sinks despite points.
3. User flags accumulate.
4. **[flagged]** — shown once users/mods flag it.
5. **[dead]** — *"killed by software, user flags, or moderators"*; hidden unless a viewer enables `showdead`.
   (HN's term — "shadowban" is community shorthand, not official.)
6. Account penalized (e.g. loss of submission privileges).
7. Account **banned** (for sockpuppet/promotional voting and rings).
8. **Cascade ban** — *"we ban their subsequent accounts as well"*; ban-evasion accounts get your main
   account banned too.
9. **Site/domain banned** — *"We've banned this site for using sockpuppet accounts, voting rings, and
   astroturfed comments."*

## What you MAY do (the entire compliant playbook)

- **Submit it yourself** as a proper Show HN (see `show-hn-rules.md`).
- **Be present in the thread** — answer questions and take feedback in good faith (HN requires the project
  be *"something you've worked on personally and which you're around to discuss"*).
- **Share it honestly** — you can tell people the thing exists / post the link on your own channels as
  information; let anyone who genuinely finds it interesting decide on their own whether to vote. Do not
  frame it as "come upvote/support".
- **Take your chances with HN's randomness** — if a solid post got no attention, a couple of reposts over
  time is ok (don't abuse it; see `postlaunch.md`).
- If you were **unfairly** ring-flagged, email `hn@ycombinator.com` — mods do restore genuine work.

## The banned-phrase scan (run over the post, first comment, and any sharing message)

Scan for (case-insensitive). **Any hit → rewrite.**

`upvote`, `up-vote`, `up vote`, `vote for`, `give us a vote`, `support us`, `support my launch`, `check out
my launch` (when aimed at votes), `help us hit`, `help me get to the front page`, `please vote`, `smash
that`, any ask tied to a vote, and any plan to use a second account or a coordinated blast.

> Note: it is fine to *say you launched* ("I built X, here's what it does, feedback welcome"). The
> violation is **asking for the vote/comment** or **coordinating** it.
