# Launch-Day Runbook (hour-by-hour, Pacific time)

Load at Step 4 for the day-of timeline. The **message copy** (emails, X thread, community posts, 1:1 DMs)
lives in the `launch-outreach` skill; this runbook is the **schedule, engagement discipline, roles, and
hard rules**. PH officially mandates only two things — go live **12:01am PT** and post a **maker first
comment** — so treat the specific clock times below as a strong recommended cadence, not PH policy.

## The core idea: feed velocity in waves

Rank rewards **sustained velocity**, not a single midnight spike. So **stagger** outreach across the day
(see `launch-outreach`'s wave plan): even pacing out-ranks a front-loaded blast, and a concentrated
burst (one geography, new accounts) looks like manipulation and can get votes stripped or the post
demoted. Hold a reserve for the midday lull and final hours.

## Hour-by-hour (Pacific Time)

| Time (PT) | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **12:01am** | Confirm the post is live; immediately **paste the pre-written maker first comment** and pin it + key resources (demo link, roadmap, a *non-vote* offer). The first comment is the single highest-leverage action — 70% of Product-of-the-Day/Week/Month winners had one. |
| **12:05–12:20am** | Notify your **closest circle only** via personal **1:1 DMs**; one heads-up in 1–2 communities where you're a real member. |
| **12:30–2:00am** | **Email Wave 1** to your most-engaged / EU / early-riser segment — ask for a look + feedback, never a vote. |
| **~6:00–9:00am** | US wakes up: post the **X launch thread**; send **email Wave 2**; post a founder-voice **LinkedIn** update. |
| **mid-morning** | Reply to **every comment** fast (target < 30 min; the strongest launches reply within ~15 min through the first 6 hours). Thank supporters by name; ask follow-up questions to keep threads alive. |
| **~12:00pm (midday lull)** | The predictable dip. Pull your rank. If momentum slipped, fire **email Wave 3** (a held-back segment) + a **"midday update" maker comment** summarizing feedback themes. |
| **12–6pm** | Peak US-Eastern traffic — stay on comments. |
| **~5:00–7:00pm** | Evening push: a "what we've learned" social post; a final **"last hours, live until midnight PT"** email to non-openers. |
| **~9:00–11:00pm** | Final-hours rally to remaining supporters; post a **day-wrap thank-you** maker comment with themes + thanks. |
| **11:59pm** | The 24-hour cycle closes. |

## Reply to every comment, fast and graciously

Comment velocity and maker responsiveness correlate strongly with final placement. Target a reply to
**every** comment within ~30 minutes. Answer feedback, bugs, and criticism separately and graciously —
**converting a skeptic in public is worth more than a defensive win**. Never argue; each genuine reply is
fresh activity that helps.

## Social amplification cadence

X thread in the US-morning wave; LinkedIn in the morning (founder voice; put the PH link in the first
comment, not the post body); targeted shares only in communities where you genuinely participate. **Many
communities forbid or restrict launch links** — confirm the rules, use the designated promo channel, and
keep a high genuine-to-promo ratio (Reddit norm ranges from ~4:1 to the older ~9:1). New-account link
drops get removed/banned. The message copy for all of these lives in `launch-outreach`.

## Team roles (if there's a team)

- **Maker/founder voice** — owns the first comment and all replies (voice consistency matters).
- **Comms/social** — runs the X thread, LinkedIn, and scheduled email waves.
- **Community** — works the Slack/Discord/Reddit channels they personally belong to.
- **Rank-watcher** — monitors placement and calls the next wave.

## Hard rules (never)

Per PH's Community Guidelines — *"Mass messaging users, asking for upvotes, using bots, incentivizing
upvotes, and any other form of artificially increasing activity ... is not acceptable"* — and the penalty
can demote or delist the post. So on launch day:

- Never say "upvote us" / "an upvote would mean everything" — say "check it out / I'd love your feedback".
- No buying votes, vote-swaps, or incentives tied to voting (a discount for **everyone** is fine; a
  discount **for voting** is not).
- No identical mass DMs to strangers; no surges from brand-new accounts or one geography.

See `policy-compliance.md` for the full prohibited list and the banned-phrase scan.
