# Product Hunt Policy & Compliance

The compliance backbone. Load when writing outreach (Step 5) and running the gate (Step 7). **This skill
coaches compliant promotion only** — if the user asks for vote manipulation, decline that part and redirect
here.

All quoted rules are verbatim from official Product Hunt surfaces (Community Guidelines; "Can I ask my
community/friends/family to upvote a product?"; "How does Product Hunt ensure fair voting…"; the Launch
Guide's "Sharing your launch" page), verified mid-2026.

## The one rule that matters most

> **"Don't ask for upvotes. Ask for feedback, comments, opinions, help spreading the word, not for
> upvotes."** — PH, *Sharing your launch*

PH's philosophy: *"People should upvote things they genuinely like or find interesting, not because they
were peer pressured to do so."* And the teeth: *"asking or incentivizing people to upvote may trigger the
algorithm to drop the product in the ranks or remove it from the homepage entirely."*

**Ask for the visit, the comment, the feedback — never for the vote.** That single line keeps every
message this skill writes compliant.

## What PH explicitly prohibits (verbatim)

> *"Mass messaging users, asking for upvotes, using bots, incentivizing upvotes, and any other form of
> artificially increasing activity on your contribution is not acceptable. Only genuine activity will be
> accepted on the site."* — Community Guidelines

> *"Don't run contests that require upvotes or reward people for upvotes."* — Sharing your launch

> *"Don't spam people. Sending hundreds of people the same tweet or scraping and sending unsolicited
> emails."* — Sharing your launch

## Prohibited-behavior checklist (NEVER do these)

- [ ] Ask directly for upvotes — in a tweet, DM, email, the maker comment, or anywhere.
- [ ] Incentivize or bribe votes, or run upvote contests/giveaways.
- [ ] Buy votes, or use upvote-exchange / vote-pod / vote-ring services.
- [ ] Use bots or any tool that artificially inflates activity.
- [ ] Create or use fake/duplicate/sock accounts to self-upvote.
- [ ] Use a **branded/company account** to post, comment, or vote (must be a real individual).
- [ ] Mass-message: identical tweets/DMs to hundreds, or scraped unsolicited cold emails.
- [ ] Self-promote in comments, or drive coordinated low-quality traffic surges.

**Consequence ladder if you do:** votes discounted/removed → drop in rank → removed from homepage /
unfeatured → contributions removed → loss of contribution & voting access → account suspension. PH's
launch team also states *"we may ask you to stop and remove any social posts"* and *"we may unfeature
your product."*

## Safe vs unsafe phrasing

| ✅ SAFE (announce + invite engagement) | ❌ UNSAFE (vote solicitation — prohibited) |
|----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| "We're live on Product Hunt today! Would love your honest feedback." | "Upvote us on Product Hunt!" / "Please upvote" |
| "Just launched — check it out and tell me what you think." | "Hunt us up" / "Smash that upvote" |
| "Come say hi and tell us what to build next." | "Upvote and I'll send you X" (incentivized) |
| "If you've used X, I'd love your thoughts in the comments." | "Vote for us to win [prize]" |
| "Help us spread the word" / "join the discussion" | "Reply DONE and I'll send the link so you can upvote" |

PH's own approved framing for what you MAY ask: *"spread the word and bring friends into the
discussion"* / ask them to *"visit and comment."*

## The banned-phrase scan (used by the gate)

Before any message ships, scan it for these tokens (case-insensitive). **Any hit = rewrite.**

`upvote`, `up-vote`, `up vote`, `vote for us`, `vote for me`, `give us a vote`, `support us` (vote sense),
`help us hit #1`, `help us win`, `an upvote would mean`, `smash that`, `hunt us up`, `click to upvote`, any
"do X and I'll give you Y" tied to voting.

> Note: it is fine to *mention* that you launched on Product Hunt; the violation is **asking for the
> vote** or **incentivizing** it. "I'd love your feedback on our PH launch" is safe; "please upvote our PH
> launch" is not.

## Accounts & identity

- Profiles must represent a **real individual**: full (first + last) name, a clear personal photo, and a
  personal bio. **Branded/company accounts** *"are not permitted to post products, comment, or vote."*
- *"Incomplete or misleading profiles may result in a higher chance of your contributions being reviewed,
  limited visibility, and/or the removal of contributions from the site."*
- Make sure the maker and any team members commenting have **complete, real, aged** accounts before
  launch day — brand-new accounts created for the launch are weighted down and can be filtered.

## How detection works (and what's inference)

PH keeps specifics deliberately vague. **Official:** *"Advanced algorithms monitor unusual voting
patterns, repetitive behavior, and potential spam,"* using *"a combination of automated tools, community
participation, and manual efforts"*; PH *"monitors point fluctuations during a launch"* and *"removes any
votes deemed non-genuine."*

**Widely reported by founders/agencies, but NOT officially confirmed (treat as low-confidence):** votes
from very new accounts (< ~72h / < ~90 days) are heavily discounted; coordinated surges and traffic from
upvote-exchange sites get throttled; public vote counts visibly **drop mid-launch** as flagged votes are
cleared. One founder reported a roughly *"2:1 ratio of upvotes to points"* from new supporters and that
bulk outreach caused PH to *"throttle my points."* Use these as reasons to **build genuine, aged-account
engagement** — not as a formula to game.

> Terminology: founders say *"shadowban,"* but PH's own words are **remove**, **unfeature**, **limited
> visibility**, **drop in the ranks**, and **loss of contribution access**. Use PH's terms when advising.

## Hunter etiquette (now simple)

PH *"encourage[s] makers to hunt their own products, and there's no discernible advantage to using a
third-party hunter."* Self-hunt by default. The etiquette reduces to one rule: **promote authentically,
drive people to engage and give feedback, never solicit or incentivize votes.**
