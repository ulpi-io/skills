# How Product Hunt Works (mechanics, timing, realistic outcomes)

Load at Step 2 to choose the date, set the hunter decision, and set honest targets. Verified mid-2026
against PH's official pages where possible; third-party estimates are labeled as such. PH keeps the exact
ranking formula opaque on purpose ("doing so would make it easier to be gamed"), so treat all weightings
as informed consensus, not spec.

## The two-stage funnel (this is the mental model)

1. **Curation → "Featured" or "All."** Human editorial curation decides whether a launch is **Featured**
   at all. Only Featured launches appear on the homepage, the mobile app, and the newsletter; non-featured
   "All" launches get **~70% less traffic** *(third-party estimate, Tetriz.io/awesome-directories — not a
   PH-published number)*. Curation tightened dramatically across 2024–2025; commonly cited figures put
   today's featured rate at **~10% of daily launches** *(third-party)*. You **cannot buy your way past
   curation with votes** — it's judged on PH's four official criteria, and whole categories are ineligible.
2. **Ranking → the leaderboard.** Among Featured launches, the daily leaderboard sorts by **velocity and
   engagement quality**, not raw vote totals.

### PH's official featuring criteria

A launch is judged on four human dimensions — **Useful, Novel, High Craft, Creative** — and only needs to
spike on one or two. There is **no public minimum vote or review count** to be featured. Commonly
**ineligible**: waitlists, directories, templates, courses, podcasts, most hardware, and services.

## The launch-day window (official)

- Launch day is a **24-hour cycle on Pacific Time**; the leaderboard **resets at midnight PT**.
- PH's official guidance: *"12:01 am Pacific Time is the best time to launch for makers that are planning
  ahead"* — it gives the full 24 hours to accumulate engagement. (PH writes **"PST"**; the wall-clock
  12:01am shifts one UTC hour seasonally since PH tracks America/Los_Angeles — **PST** in winter, **PDT**
  in summer.)
- PH does **not** convert to the visitor's timezone — **schedule in Pacific yourself.**
- PH also hedges: *"the best day to launch is the day on which you're most prepared."* Readiness beats a
  "perfect" date.

## What actually moves rank (priority order)

1. **Upvote velocity in the first ~4 hours** (rate, not cumulative count) — front-load warm support into
   the **first 4–6 Pacific hours**. The early window is widely described as make-or-break.
2. **Voter credibility** — votes from established, active, verified accounts count more than brand-new
   ones; accounts created for the launch are discounted. *(The specific multipliers floating online —
   "30–50× a new account", "1 comment ≈ 40–50 upvotes" — are unverified blog reverse-engineering; do not
   cite them as fact.)* Practical takeaway: **don't mass-recruit fresh signups to vote.**
3. **Comments + deep threads** — a live, maker-driven conversation is a real multiplier; reply fast and
   reply deep.
4. **Time-of-day decay** — early-day engagement is worth more than late-day.
5. **Reviews** — not a featuring gate, but central to conversion and to the new Orbit Awards (below).

> The **maker first comment** is the one lever PH itself backs with data: *"70% of products who achieved
> Product of the Day, Week, or Month had a first comment by the maker."* Post it immediately, and **ask
> for feedback, NOT upvotes** (soliciting upvotes violates PH rules — see `policy-compliance.md`).

## Awards landscape (changed late 2025)

- **Daily:** Product of the Day — the badge is permanent social proof.
- **Rolling:** Weekly / Monthly / Yearly ("Best of") leaderboards by votes + engagement.
- **Golden Kitty Awards: discontinued** (announced ~Nov 14, 2025, after a 10-year run).
- **Replaced by the Orbit Awards** — **quarterly, category-based**, judged on **post-launch traction where
  verified reviews are the main signal** (extra weight on detailed reviews and founder/maker reviews),
  plus community insight and hands-on testing. First edition: Dec 1, 2025 (AI Dictation). **Implication:**
  invest in soliciting high-quality reviews in your category *after* launch day, not just launch-day votes.

## Choosing the day

- **Default: Tuesday–Thursday (Wednesday most cited)** — most traffic and the largest pool of active
  voters. *(Midweek "~30–50% more voters" is a single-blog illustration, not PH data.)*
- **Weekend counter-play:** Saturday/Sunday have far less competition, so a smaller product can place
  higher in a smaller field — but with less total traffic. Honest framing: **a #3 on a busy Wednesday
  usually beats a #1 on a quiet Sunday**, because the prize is traffic and credibility, not the badge.
- **Avoid** Mondays, Fridays, late December, major holiday weeks, the summer lull, and any day a known
  mega-launch will dominate. (Seasonal "Feb–Mar / Sep–Oct are strong" is commonly cited but unverified;
  the safe, agreed part is **avoid holidays and dead weeks**.)

## The hunter decision

**Self-hunt by default.** PH officially: *"there's no discernible advantage to using a third-party
hunter"* and it encourages makers to hunt their own products. (~79% of featured posts and ~60% of #1
Product-of-the-Day winners are self-hunted, per community data.) A well-known hunter adds a sliver of
social proof but is neither necessary nor sufficient — don't delay a launch chasing one.

## Realistic outcomes (directional — verify same-day)

Upvote thresholds shift with daily competition; confirm live on a tracker like hunted.space on the day.

| Outcome | Upvotes (directional) | Reality |
|---------|----------------------|---------|
| Top 5 / front page | ~300–600+ | Depends on the day's field |
| Product of the Day (#1) | often ~1,000+, but won at ~500 on lighter days | Raw count does **not** guarantee rank |
| Product of the Month (#1) | ~2,000+ | — |

**Traffic is the more reliable prize than upvotes:** bottom of front page ≈ ~2,000+ unique visitors in a
day; #1 ≈ ~10,000+ uniques. It is a **one-day spike, not a growth engine.** Convert it: capture **~1,000+
emails**, harvest the **backlink/SEO** (PH's domain rating is ~91), and bank the **social proof**. Upvotes
themselves are largely a vanity metric.

## Myths & failure modes to avoid

- **PH is primary customer acquisition / will go viral.** No — it *amplifies* existing momentum; it is the
  *second* channel you use once you have a small starting set. Prime a warm list first (~100–300 warm
  contacts; Demand Curve cites ≥400 as a target, not a floor).
- **You need a famous hunter.** No (see above).
- **Raw upvote count wins.** No — ~1,200-vote launches have lost to ~800-vote launches on engagement depth
  and voter credibility.
- **Vote manipulation / vote-begging.** Banned and detected — see `policy-compliance.md`.
- **Launching unfinished, neglecting the first comment, or going silent on launch day.** Each is a
  self-inflicted loss; the day rewards a polished demo and a responsive maker.
